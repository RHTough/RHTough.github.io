About Me

Riley Tough is a PhD-trained bioinformatician with 8 years of experience analyzing high-dimensional genomic and transcriptomic data, developing scalable pipelines, and applying statistical and machine learning models to health and biological datasets. He is experienced with cloud and HPC environments, published manuscripts in Nature and Scientific Reports, and recently trained in deep learning and applied AI. He uses his proven ability to lead multidisciplinary projects, communicate insights effectively, and support evidence-based decision-making to generate actionable insights in health and life sciences.

Projects

  • Bioinformatics Toolkit: A reproducible R Markdown package for training new lab staff in genomics workflows.
  • HIV Set Point GWAS: Co-authored a Nature-published study on host genetic drivers of HIV viral load in African populations.
